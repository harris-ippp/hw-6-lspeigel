The Wonderful World of Sushi

Narezushi, a form of fermented sushi, is thought to be the original form of sushi that originated in Japan around the 3rd century BCE. Pre-refrigeration, fish could not be preserved to be eaten raw, so fermentation was the only way to preserve fish. Fermentation is common technique found in Japanese cooking, and sushi originated as a way to store excess fish that could not be consumed immediately. After salting and pickling the fish, the belly would be stuffed with rice, and then packaged together in wooden barrels that would sit for a year.
